Orion

The Great Hunter

Northern & Southern Hemispheres
December - April

In Greek mythology, Orion was a great hunter, tutored by Artemis, Goddess of the Hunt herself. Accounts of his death vary, though a common myth recounts that he was killed by a scorpion, and after his death he was placed among the stars with his two hunting dogs, Canis major and Canis minor. One of the most identifiable constellations, Orion can be seen with his shield and club in-hand, and a sword at his waist. In conjunction with the story of his death, he is pursued in the sky by the constellation Scorpius.
