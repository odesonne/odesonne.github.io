Brush Script Std | Medium

Designed in 1942 by Robert E. Smith, Brush Script has a “spontaneous, brush-written look,” emphasized by the connected and irregular nature of the lowercase letters. The font was originally created using a long soft brush known as a lettering quill, creating the contrast in line thickness and softening the changes in direction of the letterforms. Popular in the 1950s, Brush Script still provides the lettering for the iconic Ed Sullivan Theatre in New York City.
