Saturn

Gem of the Solar System

Northern & Southern Hemispheres
sixth planet from star Sol

Saturn is arguably the most beautiful planet in our solar system, with its extensive ring system and rich tawny color. The planet is named after the Roman god Saturnus, who is conflated with the Greek titan Kronos. Kronos went on to sire many of the famous Greek gods, such as Zeua, Hera, Hades, and Poseidon. The gas giant is comprised largely of hydrogen and helium, with 62 confirmed moons in orbit, the largest of which is Titan.

Saturn's Rings

Saturn has seven primary rings, spanning about 175,000 miles in diameter, composed of primarily of ice and rock. The most extensive ring system in our Solar System, Saturn's rings do not have many gaps between them, but are rather a series of concentric rings with banding of different densities of materials.
